I am a fourth-year Ph.D. student in Computer Science at <a href="https://www.vt.edu/">Virginia Tech</a>, mentored by <a href="https://shaddih.com/">Professor Dr. Shaddi Hasan</a>. My research focuses on <span style="color:MediumSeaGreen">Internet Measurement</span>, <span style="color:MediumSeaGreen">Broadband Infrastructure Analysis</span>, and <span style="color:MediumSeaGreen">Internet Data Analytics</span>, with a passion for solving real-world challenges in <span style="color:MediumSeaGreen">Computer Networks</span> and <span style="color:MediumSeaGreen">Information and Communication Technologies for Development (ICTD)</span>. I study how Internet infrastructure and routing behavior affect the connectivity and performance that <span style="color:MediumSeaGreen">end users</span> experience in practice. My work sits at the intersection of <span style="color:MediumSeaGreen">Internet Measurement</span>, <span style="color:MediumSeaGreen">Quality of Experience (QoE)</span>, and <span style="color:MediumSeaGreen">data-driven network analysis</span>, using large-scale measurement data to better understand network performance, topology, and accessibility.

My expertise combines a strong foundation in <span style="color:MediumSeaGreen">Statistical Methodologies</span> with practical applications of <span style="color:MediumSeaGreen">Machine Learning</span> for analyzing large-scale Internet measurement datasets. I use these tools to investigate broadband accessibility, validate network infrastructure data, and uncover patterns in Internet performance across regions and operators. My recent research, presented at <a href="https://conferences.sigcomm.org/imc/2024/">ACM IMC 2024</a>, introduced the <em>Broadband Integrity</em> project (paper: <em>“Red is Sus”</em>), which analyzes measurement data to identify discrepancies in the <a href="https://broadbandmap.fcc.gov/">U.S. National Broadband Map</a>. This work develops automated statistical and machine learning methods to detect questionable service availability claims by ISPs and provide actionable insights to support <span style="color:MediumSeaGreen">Broadband Transparency</span> and <span style="color:MediumSeaGreen">Broadband Equity</span>.

Building on this work, I am currently developing <span style="color:MediumSeaGreen">NetBreakout</span>, a measurement-driven approach for mapping mobile network egress topology using large-scale traceroute datasets. This project aims to identify where traffic exits mobile operator networks and how those paths evolve over time, helping researchers better understand the structure of the Internet from an <span style="color:MediumSeaGreen">end-user measurement perspective</span>. I am also interested in <span style="color:MediumSeaGreen">IP geolocation validation</span>, developing measurement-based techniques that leverage traceroutes and network topology information to evaluate and improve the accuracy of geolocation databases widely used by measurement platforms and Internet services.

I am particularly motivated by societal challenges such as improving broadband access, enhancing end-user network experiences, and supporting data-driven policymaking. My prior experience as a <span style="color:MediumSeaGreen">Network Planner</span> has provided valuable practical insights into mobile and broadband infrastructure, which I integrate into my research to bridge the gap between <span style="color:MediumSeaGreen">network measurement research</span> and real-world network operations.

Through my work, I aim to develop measurement methodologies and analytical tools that help researchers, network operators, and policymakers better understand Internet infrastructure and performance. Ultimately, my goal is to advance <span style="color:MediumSeaGreen">Internet transparency</span>, improve <span style="color:MediumSeaGreen">network observability</span>, and support policies that promote <span style="color:MediumSeaGreen">Digital Equity</span> in the global Internet ecosystem.
